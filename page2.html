Director's letter 

Grown up in the socialist society of 20th century and spent my childhood filled with the ideological belief that public property is more valuable than human right, I often suffocate with the fact that the human right is still a rhetoric for the 21st democratic society. I feel desperate realizing that the highly educated and skilled people are tend to be treated unfairly and not getting equal opportunity in our society with the mantra “networking is a real power”, unless they do not have influential relationship. This feeling was a primary motive for producing our serial “Victim”. Although a goal cannot be an excuse for an action, people tend to feel themselves as a victim when they are being treated unfairly for their appropriate approach. Consequently, they start to wish others to suffer the same as themselves and losing their humanity, letting go of their human nature and tend to be selfish in everything. Morality is so vulnerable and fragile.

Producer's note

Mongolian cinema is still a closed book to the international audiences. Filmmakers have been struggling to shape the modern cinemas progressively in last 30 years. Their biggest challenge is finding a soul of artistic style that differentiates their cinema from the rest. Often, when outsiders think of Mongolia, they imagine the beautiful wide steppe, Mongolian horses and Chingis Khan’s heroic story. We do take pride in all of these. However, I personally want to share a modern story with international audiences. The limited series “Victim”, is one that I have been privileged enough to be involved from the initial development. It seeks to reveal conflicts of modern Mongolian society through questioning its characters’ internal moral battles as well as external unexpected challenges as well. I believe you can experience different layers of life in the emerging country of Mongolia.
